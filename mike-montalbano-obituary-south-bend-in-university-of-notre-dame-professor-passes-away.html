Mike Montalbano Obituary; South Bend IN, University Of Notre Dame Professor Passes Away.

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, June 9, 2024

We regret to notify you of the passing of South Bend, Indiana, educator and cherished member of the Notre Dame community, Professor Mike Montalbano. The obituary of Mike Montalbano pays homage to a life well-lived, one that touched the hearts and brains of a great number of friends, coworkers, and students. Born in South Bend on April 15, 1950, Professor Montalbano dedicated his life to serving his community and the institution he held dear.

He returned to teaching at Notre Dame after earning his PhD in history from the University of Michigan. Throughout his whole career, he was committed to encouraging his studentsâ€™ innate curiosity and critical thinking. His dedication to the library matched his zeal for the classroom, and he received widespread recognition for his contributions to the study of American history. In addition to his intelligence, Mike Montalbano will be remembered for his compassion and kindness.

He had an extraordinary gift for relating to others and being able to provide them with direction, solace, and friendship. He loved to tell tales and would frequently regale his audience with narratives that brought the past to life. His compassion and goodwill were not limited to the classroom; he participated in many community events and mentored many people.

Professor Montalbano had an impact that went much beyond the university. Both his family, who cherished his love and wisdom, and the South Bend community, where he actively participated in civic organizations, are very saddened by his passing. Those who had the good fortune to know him will always treasure his legacy. To sum up, Mike Montalbano was a brilliant man who will be missed by South Bend, the University of Notre Dame, and the academic community. His legacy of academic achievement, mentoring, and altruism will inspire the next generation. We shall always cherish his contributions to education and his kind disposition.
